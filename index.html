Effect on Corporations

"ISPs will celebrate the regulations surrounding the [internet] being disolved. They can now implement paid prioiritization services. Regulation is now up to big telecommunication corporations and their 'promise to preserve the open internet'."
- Irfan Jafrey

Effect on Every Day Life

"Access to your favorite sites like Netflix and Youtube can slow if the companies chose not to pay the ISPs. You may also be asked to pay more for access to websites that you use on a daily basis."
- Daniel Kim, Computer Science student at UC Berkeley

Effect on Small Business

"The bounce rate for websites is 9.6% if your site takes 2 seconds to load. But at 4 seconds, the bounce rate is 17.1%. Since you will likely be outbided by a larger competitor, you will lose out on search engine marketing and website optimization."
- Peter Banerjea

Effect on Information

"Access to ideas that your internet service provider disagrees with would be harder. For example, under the repeal of net neutrality laws, ISPs can now throttle the searches for 'Why Net Neutrality is Bad?'"
- Joaquin Frommer, Cybersecurity Student at Northeastern University
